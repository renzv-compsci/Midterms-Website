Freedom in the Lines

The page no longer haunts my hand,
Its white expanse, a promised land.
With every stroke, I breathe in deep,
No fear to wake, no doubt to keep.

My heart now races, not from fear,
But from the joy of what draws near.
Each line, a dance, unchained, untamed,
A city born, its beauty claimed.

The world, it bends beneath my view,
A canvas wide, in shades of blue.
No longer bound by perfect form,
I sketch with ease, in passion's storm.

An artist’s eye, I now embrace,
Each crooked line, each empty space.
For in the flaws, I see the grace—
A city’s soul, in every trace.

Lines of Fear, Shades of Growth

At first, the page was vast and cold,
My hands unsure, the pen too bold.
A city’s edge, a daunting sight,
My heart, it trembled in the light.

Each stroke, a whisper of my doubt,
The fear within, I traced it out.
Yet as the ink began to flow,
The city’s soul began to show.

With every line, the fear would fade,
A world from shadows I had made.
Now, what was once a timid start,
Has grown to fill a cornered heart.

And every day, I sketch anew,
The love for this—the lines I drew.
No longer bound by anxious ties,
I see the world through artist’s eyes.
